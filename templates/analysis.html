Key Academic Findings & Methodology Brief

The AI Curve Hypothesis (Quadratic Decay)

Through regression fitting, we modeled the non-linear relationship of AI engagement hours on cognitive outcomes. The equation dictates that learning support tools act as positive cognitive scaffolds up to a threshold of 10-12 hours per week. Beyond this apex, the slope turns negative. The drop correlates with "outsourced learning" where students generate solutions without mental synthesis.

Variable Importances and Multicollinearity

Traditional study hours and prior GPA remain high anchors of academic success. Although AI usage offers a minor leverage point, a student's underlying study habits are a prerequisite. The correlation matrix suggests that students with high GPAs tend to use AI more effectively (correlation coefficient of GPA with Concept Explanations and Coding), utilizing the tools as expert tutors rather than short-cut generators.
